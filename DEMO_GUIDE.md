## Demo Script (5-7 minutes)

### 1. Landing Page (30 seconds)

**What to show:**
- Clean, professional design
- Clear value proposition: "Discover Cannabis with Clarity"
- Three distinct paths: Learn, Medical, Recreational

**What to say:**
> "Verdant is a cannabis discovery platform that aggregates information from existing sources like Leafly and Weedmaps. We're not selling anything - we're the search layer that helps users find exactly what they need, then directs them to established platforms for purchase."

**Action**: Briefly hover over the three path cards to show interaction

---

### 2. Educational Content (45 seconds)

**What to show:**
Click "Learn" to open the education page

**What to say:**
> "A significant portion of our users will be first-timers or people with questions. We provide comprehensive, accessible education on cannabinoids, consumption methods, strain types, dosing, and safety. This positions us as a trusted, responsible platform."

**Action**: Scroll through to show the depth of content (don't read everything)

---

### 3. Medical Products (90 seconds)

**What to show:**
Click "Medical" from the navigation

**What to say:**
> "The medical section focuses on CBD products for therapeutic use. Let me show you how the filtering works."

**Actions:**
1. Click the "anxiety" filter tag
2. Click "High-CBD" under CBD Content
3. Show the filtered results: "Now we're showing only high-CBD products effective for anxiety"
4. Click on a product (e.g., "Charlotte's Web")

**On the product detail page:**
> "This is where our aggregation adds real value. We're showing sentiment analysis across thousands of reviews from multiple platforms, common user feedback, and direct links to the original sources. Users can make informed decisions based on consolidated data."

**Action**: Scroll to show the "View on Partner Platforms" section with Leafly/Weedmaps links

---

### 4. Recreational Products (90 seconds)

**What to show:**
Navigate to "Recreational" from the header

**What to say:**
> "The recreational side uses different filters - focusing on effects, mood, and potency rather than medical conditions."

**Actions:**
1. Type "energy" in the search bar and search
2. Click the "High (23%+)" THC potency filter
3. Show results: "Here are high-potency sativa strains for energy and focus"
4. Click on a product (e.g., "Sour Diesel" or "Green Crack")

**On the product page:**
> "Same comprehensive aggregation - ratings from multiple platforms, user sentiment, and links to where they can actually purchase. We handle discovery; established platforms handle transactions and compliance."

---

### 5. Comparison Tool (60 seconds)

**What to show:**
Click "Compare Products" button on any browse page

**What to say:**
> "Users can compare up to three products side-by-side - useful when trying to decide between similar options."

**Actions:**
1. Search for a product type (e.g., "indica")
2. Add 2-3 products to comparison
3. Show the side-by-side comparison view

**What to say:**
> "This makes it easy to see differences in potency, effects, price, and user ratings at a glance."

---

### 6. Closing Points (60 seconds)

**What to emphasize:**

1. **No Competition**: 
   > "We're not competing with Leafly or Weedmaps - we're sending them traffic. We're solving a different problem: aggregating across platforms so users don't have to check five different sites."

2. **Scalability**:
   > "This structure works for 50 products or 50,000. The filtering system is flexible, the data model is extensible."

3. **Market Approach**:
   > "We'd start focused - maybe Germany's emerging market, or one US state - validate the model, then expand. Lower risk than trying to launch everywhere at once."

4. **Revenue Later**:
   > "Revenue model is phase two. First, we build something users love and trust. Referral fees, advertising, premium features - lots of options once we have traffic."

5. **Responsible Positioning**:
   > "Notice we're not making medical claims, we're citing sources, we have educational content, clear disclaimers. This is a responsible, information-first approach."

## Common Questions & Answers

**Q: How do you get the data?**
A: Web scraping and APIs from public sources. In production, we'd maintain partnerships with major platforms for cleaner data access.

**Q: What about regulations?**
A: Since we don't sell products, we avoid most transaction-related regulations. We're purely informational with appropriate disclaimers. Legal review needed for each market we enter.

**Q: How is this different from Leafly?**
A: Leafly is a marketplace and community for one platform. We're a meta-layer that searches across ALL platforms - Leafly, Weedmaps, Reddit, local dispensary sites, etc. We make the whole ecosystem more accessible.

**Q: Revenue model?**
A: Several paths: referral fees (paid per click-through), advertising from dispensaries/brands, premium features for power users, B2B licensing to dispensaries for our filtering tech.

**Q: What's the MVP timeline?**
A: With seed funding, 3-4 months to production-ready v1 focused on one market. Another 2-3 months to refine based on user feedback.

**Q: Competition?**
A: No direct competitors doing cross-platform aggregation. Leafly and Weedmaps are walled gardens. We're creating a new category.
